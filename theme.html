Appearance as a Faulty Measure of Reality

  • Julian’s mother thinks that her and her son are superior to the black people around her
    • However, the purple hat that both she and the black mother wears shows that they are not much different from one another
  • Julian’s mother uses public demeanor and appearance to deviate from those lower than her
      • Shows with her insistence on looking good and social conduct
  • Julian uses college as a reason that he supports racial relations and integration
    • However, he still judges other black people just the same like his mother
    • He is not comfortable talking to other black people
    • Thinks of black people as an objective

Thematic Sentence
Different generations of people, especially with parents and children, with conflicting views will clash which will lead to dismay.

“Knowing who you are is good for one generation only.” (O’Connor). Julian does not believe identity and customs are not useful. He further elaborates on this idea by stating: “the old manners are obsolete and your graciousness is not worth a damn” after Julian’s mother tries to remain civil in an interaction with a person of different race(O’Connor). He believes that it is useless, but because of the generation difference between him and his mother, they do not view the issue the same.
