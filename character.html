Characterization

  • Definition of a closet racist
    • Finds it hard to talk to black people
    • Stops his mother from giving a penny to a black child
    • Secretly judges the black mother
  • Seems to only see black people as an objective to get his mother mad
    • Justifies this as his mother being in the “old world” and him being wise with liberal beliefs of integration and racial relations which he got from college.
  • Gets mad when he does not get to see the reaction he wants from his mother
  • Does not want to help out his mother with everyday activities
    • Does not want to take his mother to the weight-reducing classes at the Y
  • Failed a writer and currently selling typewriters
  • Acts like an undisciplined brat
  • Acts openly racist
    • hates integration
    • Wants people of color to stay on their side of the fence
    • “‘There are no more slaves,’ he said irritably. ‘They were better off when they were.’ she said” (O’Connor 384).
    • “They should rise, yes, but on their own side of the fence.” (O’Connor 384).
  • Extremely polite
      • Although, in the past, manners were considered a way to be superior
  • Thinks of the past as comfort and security
    • “‘Tell Grandpa to come get me,’ she said…’Tell Caroline to come get me,’ she said” (O’Connor).
