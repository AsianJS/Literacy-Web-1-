Point of View

Third Person Limited

  • Makes the story subjective since we are only seeing it through the perspective of Julian
  • Could create a potential bias because only the thoughts of Julian are provided
  • Details about the mother’s thoughts and motives are left out
  • Julian believes he is free of prejudice in his thoughts, but his actions prove otherwise
    • “He was free of prejudice and unafraid to face facts” (O’ Connor 388).
    • Though he feels like he is free form his mother, he still carries some of her views unintentionally
    • The prejudice Julian’s mother has is passed down to Julian subconsciously and though he does not despise African Americans, he still views them differently than any other people
    • “Everything that gave [Julian’s mother] pleasure was small and depressed [Julian].” (O’Connor).
    • The narrator challenges some of Julian’s views by providing his thoughts and his contradicting actions
